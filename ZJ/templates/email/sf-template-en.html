The aim of the attendance rate

The attendance rate is meant to prevent our Talents from canceling shifts at short notice. Until now, it made no difference to our Talents whether they canceled a shift early or last minute, as they were penalized either way. However, there was one crucial difference for you and us: if they canceled at short notice, we would potentially lack time to re-staff the shift.


The new attendance rate encourages our Talents to cancel a shift as early as possible. There are no negative consequences for cancellations more than 48 hours in advance. We now only penalize last-minute cancellations – but we do so more consistently and harshly than before.
How does the attendance rate work?
1.

The attendance rate measures how many times in the last 30 days a Talent has canceled a booked shift within 48 hours of the start time for reasons other than illness.

2.

If the attendance rate falls below 80%, the Talent cannot apply for new shifts for a certain period of time.

3.

A shift cancellation with more than 48 hours' notice will not affect the attendance rate.

The attendance rate is the next step in collaborating with you and our Talents. The feature is expected to reduce the number of shifts we cannot fill due to last-minute cancellations.
